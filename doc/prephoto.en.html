Prepare terrain photo bitmaps

Prepare terrain photo bitmaps. This process is most hard one.

Get the photo

First, get aerial or satellite photo bitmaps from any vendors then scan the photo by scanners.

At least 4.8m/pixel resolution is needed.

Correct photo

If the whole photo scenery area is very huge, You must correct the photo.

To do this, get accurate topographical maps, overlaps the photo texture over the maps with layer function of any graphic processing software like Adobe Photo Shop, Paint Shop Pro etc.

Note: You must use "Unprojected Maps" as topographical maps:


"Map Projection Overview", Peter H. Dana.

Unprojected maps include those that are formed by considering longitude and latitude as a simple rectangular coordinate system. Scale, distance, area, and shape are all distorted with the distortion increasing toward the poles.

Alpha (water-mask) bitmap

You need alpha bitmap to 'hollow out' the water area.

See sample bitmaps in Custom Terrain Textures SDK.

Bitmap format

Bitmaps format must be 24bit full-color BMP file.

If you want to use seasonal/lightmap photo scenery, you need all 6 bitmaps of spring/summer/fall/winter/harsh winter/lightmap.

Optimal resolution of the bitmap is 4.8m/pixel (North-South), and 6.4 * cos(latitude) m/pixel (East-West).
